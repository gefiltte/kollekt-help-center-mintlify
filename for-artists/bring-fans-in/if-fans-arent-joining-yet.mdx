You announced. You got five members. You thought it would be more. Before you conclude "Kollekt isn't working for me," read this. Most slow starts are fixable in an afternoon.

## Why this matters

A quiet first week makes most artists bail on the space. But the numbers right after a first announcement are rarely a signal about Kollekt — they're a signal about the announcement. Instagram Stories are seen by maybe 5–10% of your followers. First-time audience moves convert 1–3% of the people who actually see them. Even a big artist will get 20–100 members in week 1. Your job now is to figure out what's off and adjust, not to give up.

## Before you panic

A reality check:

- **Instagram's reach is limited.** Your Story didn't reach everyone — it reached a slice. Most of your followers never saw it.
- **First announcements under-perform.** Fans need to see it 2–3 times before most tap through. That's normal.
- **Growth compounds with use.** Your week-12 numbers depend more on how often you're posting to Kollekt than on how big your announcement was.
- **You're competing with feed overload.** Your fans are seeing 50–100 Stories a day. Being specific and repeated beats being early.

If it's been less than a week and you have any members at all, you're not behind. You're right on track.

## What to check first

Before re-announcing, rule out the silent blockers:

- **Is your bio link live and pointing to the right URL?** Open it from an incognito browser. Does it land on your Kollekt Home page?
- **Does your Home page look ready?** Cover photo, artist name, at least one Direct Line post. If fans tapped through to an empty page, they left.
- **Was your announcement specific?** "I'm on Kollekt now" is a weaker ask than "new voice note from me there this morning." Specific beats generic every time.
- **Did you mention what fans actually get?** "Join my Kollekt" doesn't tell a fan why. "Voice notes, early music, and the stuff I don't post anywhere else" does.
- **Did you share on more than one surface?** One Instagram Story rarely does it. Did you also post to the feed? DM your close circle? Mention it on TikTok?

If any of these are off, fix them before re-announcing. The fans you're trying to reach didn't see the announcement in the first place.

## Tactical course-corrections

If the checks above are clean and you still want more members, a few moves that work:

### DM 10 close fans directly

The highest-converting thing you can do. Not a mass DM — individual messages to people you'd actually text. A template:

> Hey — made a Kollekt thing. It's where I'm putting everything from now on. Figured you'd want to be in. Here: kollekt.io/yourname. No pressure just thought of you.

10 sent 1:1 usually gets 6–8 new members. Plus those members are the ones who'll actually post in Chat.

### Re-announce with a better hook

Don't re-pitch Kollekt. Announce a *thing* that happens to live there.

- Weak: "Don't forget my Kollekt!"
- Stronger: "Just posted a voice note about the new track on my Kollekt. Tap through."
- Strongest: "Recording a live session right now — going up on Kollekt in 10 minutes."

The stronger the specific, the higher the conversion.

### Tie it to a moment

Fans tap through when there's a reason to move. Release day. Tour announce. Behind-the-scenes from a shoot. Use a natural hook that's happening anyway.

### Loop Kollekt into your next two weeks of content

- TikTok caption: "more of this on my Kollekt"
- Instagram Story after every Direct Line post: "just posted [thing] — link in bio"
- YouTube video description: "Kollekt link is first in the description"

Two weeks of this is usually enough to move the needle.

### Show your own activity

Fans subscribe to places that feel alive. If you've posted once to Direct Line, post five more times this week. Casual, short, whatever you've got. Every post is a small pulse that tells lurkers "there's actually stuff happening here."

## When to re-announce

- **At least 2 weeks since your last announcement.** Re-announcing too soon reads as pushy.
- **When you have something specific to say.** A new track, a voice note you just dropped, an event. Not a re-pitch of Kollekt itself.
- **When the space is visibly alive.** A few Direct Line posts, a Chat with some activity. New fans should arrive into a room that's warm.

## When to wait

- **If you announced in the last week.** Too soon.
- **If nothing new has happened on Kollekt.** Don't re-announce the same empty page.
- **If your Instagram traffic is otherwise low.** Wait until your next post gets traction.

## If it's still slow after two weeks

- **Check your stats for Instagram arrivals specifically.** If they're zero, the issue is your Instagram reach, not Kollekt. Try TikTok, DM fans, put the QR at your next show.
- **Audit every bio you have.** Kollekt should be in all of them with a clear label.
- **Email [support@kollekt.io](mailto:support@kollekt.io).** We can look at your stats with you and suggest the next move.
