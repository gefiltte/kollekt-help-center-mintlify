The launch moment is 24–48 hours. The next two months are where the space either becomes sticky or goes quiet. Most artists lose their momentum here — not because Kollekt stops working, but because they stop posting. Here's the rhythm that keeps fans opening the app.

## Why this matters

Fans who don't hear from you will forget. Not because they stopped caring — because Instagram and TikTok are loud and Kollekt isn't pushy. The difference between a space that compounds and one that goes silent is one thing: your cadence. Three short posts a week beats one polished message a month.

## The weekly rhythm

A pattern that works for most artists:

- **2–3 Direct Line posts a week.** A photo and a sentence counts. A voice note counts. A "should I do this or that?" counts. Low friction, high frequency.
- **One Instagram Story per week that points back to Kollekt.** Tied to something specific you just posted — not a generic "I'm on Kollekt!"
- **Drop into Chat when you feel like it.** Heart a few messages, reply to one or two, post a short update. Ten minutes, twice a week.
- **One subscriber-only post every 1–2 weeks** (when you're ready to lean into subscriptions). See [Post subscriber-only content](/for-artists/direct-line/post-subscriber-only-content).

The pattern matters more than the volume. Fans start to expect the cadence and check for it. That's the flywheel.

## Loop back from your other platforms

Your Instagram and TikTok followers are where most new members will still come from. Every time you post something to Kollekt, there's a chance to loop people over. A pattern that works:

- **Post to Direct Line first.** A voice note, a photo, a thought.
- **Within a few hours, post a Story referencing it.** "Just dropped a voice note about [thing] on my Kollekt — link in bio."
- **Add one line to your next TikTok or YouTube caption:** "more of this on my Kollekt."

The trick is that the Story or caption has to reference something specific. "I'm on Kollekt" converts worse than "new track in the Direct Line right now" every time.

## Keep the chat alive

Chat is the sticky part of your space — when fans meet other fans who care about you, they come back. Three low-effort ways to feed it:

- **Ask something in your Direct Line that invites Chat.** "What song should I play next at the next show?" — you'll see the answer appear in Chat.
- **React quickly when fans post.** Hearts count. A one-line reply counts more. The goal is to signal the room isn't empty.
- **Drop in from the road.** Backstage, soundcheck, tour bus — "just a quick hi from [city]" gets replies more reliably than anything polished.

## Signs you're in a groove

- Your Direct Line posts get heart reactions within minutes
- Chat has conversations that aren't just you
- Stats show new members arriving every week (even if it's 5–10)
- Subscribers start to trickle in without a specific push

## Signs momentum is slipping

- You haven't posted to Direct Line in 10+ days
- Chat has gone silent
- You're posting to Kollekt but not mentioning it on Instagram
- You're posting the same content on Kollekt that's already on Instagram

Most of these are fixable in an afternoon. Pick one: post a voice note, Story about it, heart five messages in Chat.

## What not to do

- **Don't force engagement.** "Comment below!" reads as a brand, not a person.
- **Don't post for the sake of posting.** If nothing's happening, post nothing. A dry week on Kollekt is fine if the next post is real.
- **Don't duplicate Instagram content.** Fans who joined Kollekt want something they don't get there. Voice notes, long thoughts, early versions, studio moments — that kind of thing.
- **Don't panic when numbers are slow.** Most spaces double or triple in month 3, not week 1. Keep the cadence; the numbers follow.
