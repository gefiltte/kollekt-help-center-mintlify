Your social bios are seen by every fan who lands on your profile. Putting Kollekt there is the permanent, always-on version of sharing your link. Takes only a few minutes to do all of them at once.

## What to know first

- **Do all of them in one sitting.** Block a few minutes, open your main platforms, update the bios in one pass. You'll forget otherwise.
- **Use the same label everywhere.** `Kollekt` or `@yourname on Kollekt`. Same text builds recognition — fans see it repeatedly and it starts to mean something.
- **The URL is `kollekt.io/yourname`.** Lowercase, no spaces.
- **Pair the link with context.** Most platforms let you add a line of bio text around the link. A short, specific line converts much better than a generic one. Examples further down.

## Where to put it

Add your Kollekt link anywhere fans can tap a link on your profile. Each platform has its own rules:

- **Instagram** — up to 5 bio links. Add Kollekt at the top. Pair with a pinned post that explains what fans will find there.
- **TikTok** — single bio link, unlocked at 1,000+ followers. Below that, put `kollekt.io/yourname` as bio text (not clickable but memorable), use a pinned video that mentions it, and say the URL out loud in your videos.
- **YouTube** — three placements worth using: the channel About page, a pinned comment under every video, and the first line of every video description.
- **X / Twitter** — one clickable URL in the bio. Add a pinned tweet that explains what Kollekt is.
- **Facebook (artist page)** — set Kollekt as your primary Website link.

Platform UIs shift, so the exact menu path can change. If something moves, search the platform's help for "add link" or "edit profile."

## Bio text that works

The line around the link matters more than the link itself. A few framings you can adapt:

- **"All you need from me in one place."**
- **"Behind-the-scenes, early music, and updates from me."**
- **"Subscriber-only content and community chat."**
- **"Tour updates, merch, and everything else."**
- **"Where I post the stuff I don't put anywhere else."**

Swap your bio text to match what's happening that week. A bio that matches the moment converts better than a static one.

## Signs it's working

Open your Kollekt stats and check the referrer breakdown over the next two weeks:

- **Instagram** should be the biggest single source if most of your audience is there.
- **TikTok** shows up if you're posting and saying the URL.
- **YouTube** traffic arrives slowly but steadily — videos keep getting discovered.
- **X / Facebook** will be smaller trickles. Still worth it because they're fans you'd otherwise miss.

If any platform shows zero arrivals after two weeks, check that the link is actually live and that your bio mentions something worth tapping through for.

## Tips

- **Re-check the bios every quarter.** Platforms move things and change link allowances. A short audit every few months keeps everything current.
- **If you only have time for one today, do Instagram.** It's the highest-volume placement for most artists.
