A show is a room full of your highest-intent fans — paid, travelled, here. Getting them into your Kollekt space before they leave is one of the highest-conversion moments you have. You'll need a QR code pointing to your Kollekt link — any free online QR generator works (point it at `kollekt.io/yourname`, download the image, keep it handy). You'll reuse the same QR everywhere.

## Where to put the QR code

Pick two or three of these for the same tour. Don't do all of them at once — it dilutes.

**On the video screens.** If the venue has screens, loop your QR between songs. 10–20 seconds is enough. Add one line of text — short and specific. A few that work:

- "Voice note from me tomorrow. Scan."
- "The stuff I don't post anywhere else."
- "Scan for tomorrow's setlist — and everything else."

**Merch table.** A small sign next to the card reader. Every purchase is already a conversion moment; you're just adding one layer. Example lines:

- "Scan for the good stuff first."
- "Where I post voice notes + new music."
- "Unreleased things live here."

**Setlist insert or flyer.** Print a small card (A6 or smaller) with the QR and a single line. Hand them out at the door or leave them at the merch table. A few that work:

- "Tonight's setlist, tomorrow morning. Scan."
- "One link for everything from me."

**Tour poster.** If you have a printed poster for the tour, put the QR in a corner with a short tag: "Find me on Kollekt → [scan]."

## Stage banter

The ask works best when it's specific. "Follow me on Kollekt" is weak. "Scan this before the last song and you'll get a voice note from me tomorrow morning" is a reason. One mention per show is enough — more than that and fans tune out. A few scripts — pick one and make it yours:

> "Before the last song — if you want voice notes, early demos, the stuff I don't post anywhere else — scan the QR on the screen. Five seconds."

> "Quick thing. Scan the QR before you leave and I'll send a voice note from tomorrow's studio day. Nothing else. Just that."

> "If you liked tonight, there's a QR on the screen. That's where I'll post the rest of the tour from — tonight's setlist, tomorrow's soundcheck, all of it."

> "One more thing. I made a Kollekt. It's where I actually talk to you, no algorithm. Scan if you want in."

## Merch table

Every fan at the merch table already raised their hand. Don't over-ask. A small sign or a sticker on the card reader does most of the work. If you have a moment of conversation, one line is enough: "If you want the new stuff first, scan this." Point at the sign.

## Tips

- **Have a Direct Line post ready for the morning after.** A voice note saying "last night was amazing, here's a thing I recorded on the way home" rewards the fans who scanned and tells them they made the right call.
- **If you don't have shows coming up soon**, a QR on your Instagram Highlights cover, or pinned in your Stories, gives you most of the same leverage in the meantime.
- **Don't ask fans to type a URL.** A fan scanning a QR from their seat is 3–4 taps from joining. Typing `kollekt.io/yourname` from memory at 11pm is nobody's best moment.
