Your first announcement reaches more people than anything else you'll post about Kollekt for months. Keep it short, make it specific, and make sure the page isn't empty when fans tap through.

## Before you announce

Two things should be true:

- Your Home has a cover photo and your artist name is right. See [Edit your Artist page](/for-artists/home/editing-the-artist-page).
- You've got at least one post in your Direct Line. A photo and a sentence is fine — just not an empty feed.

If fans tap through to an empty space they won't come back.

## What makes an announcement work

- **Point at a thing, not at Kollekt.** "New voice note on my Kollekt" converts better than "I'm on Kollekt now." A reason to tap beats an announcement every time.
- **Keep it short.** Fans are scrolling. One line gets read.
- **Sound like you.** If you post in lowercase with no punctuation, do that. An announcement that reads like a press release reads like a team wrote it.

## Where to post it

Three surfaces cover most of your audience.

### Instagram Story (with the link sticker)

The biggest-reach surface for most artists. The link sticker is the only way Instagram lets fans tap through — use it.

If you've got something to point at:
> new voice note on my kollekt → [link sticker]

If you don't yet:
> i'm on kollekt now. everything going here → [link sticker]

See [Post your Kollekt link on Instagram Story](/for-artists/bring-fans-in/instagram-stories) for the mechanical how-to.

### Instagram feed post

The pinned post that stays on your profile. A bit longer than a Story, still short.

> on kollekt now. voice notes, early versions, studio stuff — all going here. link in bio.

### Close-fan DMs

The highest-converting move, and most artists skip it. Send 1:1 messages to 10–20 people you'd actually text — not a mass DM, one at a time.

> hey — putting everything on kollekt now. figured you'd want in. kollekt.io/yourname
