Direct Line messages can carry photos, videos, and audio, with or without a text caption. You can mix media and text — a caption sits under the photo, video, or audio file. Every message still sends one push notification, whether it's a sentence or a full video.

Voice notes are a separate flow: the microphone icon records in-app, while the Audio option in the + menu uploads a file you already have. For the voice-note flow, see [Send a Direct Line message](/for-artists/direct-line/sending-messages).

## The attachment options

Tap the **+** button in the compose bar — it's to the left of the message input.

![Attachment options expanded](screenshots/directline-attachment-options.png)

Three options appear:

- **Camera** — take a new photo.
- **Media** — pick photos or videos from your library. You can select multiple at once.
- **Audio** — upload an audio file from your device.

## Send multiple photos

Pick more than one photo from your library and they stack in the composer as a horizontal row with a count badge.

![Composing with multiple photos](screenshots/directline-composer-multi-photo.png)

Add a caption if you want, then send. The photos appear in the feed as a grid.

## Send a video

Pick a video from your library. It appears in the feed with a play-button overlay. Captions work the same as photo posts.

![Video message in feed](screenshots/directline-video-message.png)

## Signs it's working

- The media preview appears in the composer before you send.
- After sending, the post shows in the feed with the photo grid, video frame, or audio player visible.
- Fans get a push notification as usual — one per message, regardless of how much media is attached.
