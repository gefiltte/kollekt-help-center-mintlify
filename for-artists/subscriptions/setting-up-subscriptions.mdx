# Setting Up Subscriptions

Subscriptions are a Phase 3 tool. They work best once you have an active community — not at the start.

<Warning>
**This article is a stub.** The full subscription setup flow is being documented from video. Reach out at [support@kollekt.io](mailto:support@kollekt.io) for hands-on help in the meantime.
</Warning>

## What subscriptions do on Kollekt

Much of the subscriber value is built into the platform — you don't have to keep creating extra content to justify the price.

- **Highlighted names in Chat.** Subscribers stand out.
- **Image and video in Chat.** Subscribers can share media, others can't.
- **Subscriber badges.** Visible markers of support.
- **Subscriber-only Direct Line posts.** Lock a post and only paying fans see it.
- **Subscriber-only chat rooms.** A separate space for the people who stepped up.

The conversion happens organically. A fan in Chat sees subscribers with highlighted names. A fan on Direct Line sees a locked post they can't read. The platform creates the pull. You just have to keep being present.

## When to turn subscriptions on

Not on day one. The rule of thumb: wait until your Chat is active on its own — fans are talking to each other without you having to seed every thread — and your Direct Line has a cadence. At that point a subscriber tier feels like a natural next step, not a sales pitch.

## Setting it up

1. Open the admin menu from your Artist Page (three-dot icon, top right).
2. Tap **Payments**.
3. Follow the prompts to set your price and confirm payout details.

Your personal profile needs to have payouts set up first. See: [Edit Your User Profile](/for-artists/user-profile/edit-user-profile).
