Subscriptions are on from day one in Kollekt — nothing to enable, nothing to unlock. Fans can subscribe from the moment you claim your space, and your earnings start accruing from the first sale.

## Pricing and your share

Subscriptions start at €4.99 a month. From there, it's pay-what-you-want — fans can choose to pay more if they want to support you further, and everyone gets the same perks regardless of what they pay.

You keep 80% of subscription revenue after VAT and Stripe fees. Kollekt takes 20%.

## A note on iOS pricing

Apple charges a fee on in-app purchases in the App Store. Kollekt passes that fee through to fans who subscribe inside the iOS app, so the starting price looks different depending on where a fan signs up:

| Where the fan subscribes | Starting price |
|---|---|
| Web (`app.kollekt.io/yourname`) | €4.99 / month |
| Android app | €4.99 / month |
| iOS app | €5.88 / month |

Your earnings are calculated from the base €4.99 either way — the extra on iOS covers Apple's fee, not your revenue. It's worth mentioning to your fans when you announce subscriptions: if they're comfortable signing up through the web or Android, they pay less for the same perks.

## What subscribers get

Most of the value is built into the platform. You don't have to keep inventing new perks — the platform creates the pull.

- **Highlighted names in Chat.** Subscribers stand out in the room.
- **Image and video in Chat.** Subscribers can share media. Other members can't.
- **Subscriber badges.** A visible marker of support next to the name.
- **Subscriber-only Direct Line posts.** Lock a post and only paying fans can open it. See [Post subscriber-only content](/for-artists/direct-line/post-subscriber-only-content).
- **Subscribers Chat room.** A separate room for the people who stepped up.

A fan in Main Chat sees subscribers with highlighted names. A fan in Direct Line sees locked posts they can't read. The platform makes the perks visible — you just have to keep showing up.

## How revenue flows

1. **A fan subscribes.** They're charged monthly at the amount they chose (€4.99 or higher, or the iOS-adjusted equivalent).
2. **Earnings accrue to your space.** No payout account needed for this — revenue is tracked regardless.
3. **You split across your team if you want.** If you've added team members, each takes their share. See [Add team members and split revenue](/for-artists/admin/team-members-and-revenue-splits).
4. **You withdraw when you're ready.** Connect your payout account any time. Earnings held until then are released on Stripe's normal payout schedule once you connect. See [Connect your payout account](/for-artists/subscriptions/connect-your-payout-account).

## When to lean into subscriptions

Not on day one. The rule of thumb: wait until your Chat is active on its own (fans talking to each other without you seeding every thread) and your Direct Line has a cadence. At that point subscriber perks feel natural, not a sales pitch.

When you're ready to push:

- Post subscriber-only content regularly. See [Post subscriber-only content](/for-artists/direct-line/post-subscriber-only-content).
- Show up in the Subscribers Chat room yourself.
- Mention the web subscription option when you announce — your fans save money and you hit the same earnings either way.

## Signs it's working

Your Stats screen shows new subscribers arriving each week, your earnings balance is rising, and fans in the Subscribers Chat are posting without you having to prompt them.
